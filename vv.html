Case Study 01

Website Design: VineVault

Paradise for wine lovers.
Finding your best-fit alcoholic beverages.

What is VineVault?

A platform for wine lovers to explore their wine journeys easily. Consumers can make online purchases, make bulk purchases, make comparisons among similar wines, choose their best-fit alcoholic beverages and keep records of their perferences.

Problem Statement

Hong Kong people are often busy and lack time to make purchases from physical stores. Wine beginners are often frustrated with the wide variety of choices and have difficulty finding their preferences.

Create a website that allows users to:

  • Make purchases conveniently without visiting the local stores.
  • Provide relevant information without the need to ask for a shop assistant.
  • Learn and make comparisons among goods of the same category

Key Findings

Confusion in the payment section

Some users have reflected that the location of privacy, return policy and contacts were unclear.

Lack of estimated delivery time

Most users thinks that the estimated delivery time should be shown upon order confirmation so that they can make better planning and have more control over the order.

Shopping cart and wishlist preview

Users reflected that it would be more efficient if they can preview the products in their shopping cart and wishlist so that they can make a quick check on what to purchase instead of naviagtion from page-to-page.

Comparison among >2 products

Some participants thinks that it may be better to compare among 3 products instead of 2 as there are too many kinds of similar products.

Solution

Improve visibility of the return policy link.

Solution

Adding the estimated delivery time upon order confirmation.

Solution

Allow users to preview the shopping cart and wishlist on the right side of the window upon single-clicking. Double-clicking for navigation to the shopping cart and wishlist.

Solution

Allow comparisons among 3 products.

Conclusion

With the usability tests, we obtained some valuable insights about the user experience of the product. To sum up, users found the initial navigation and product search to be effective and user-friendly. However, there are some challenges regarding the functional details that impact overall satisfaction and task completion rates.

The findings reveal that while the landing page, product categorization and checkout process are well-designed, the cart-to-pages navigation and policy visibility were underdeveloped. The inconvenient navigation and poor visibility may lead to low conversion rate due to low efficiency and insignificant information support.

Overall, the usability study highlighted the successful design elements while uncovering some areas for improvement. Through addressing the identified issues, the user experience can be enhanced, the customer satisfaction and conversion rates can also be increased. The development and testing stage will be iterated continuously in the future to ensure that the design effectively meets user needs and keep up with the trend.
